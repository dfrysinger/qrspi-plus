feat(qrspi-task-03): add per-phase visual AC precondition assertion when visual_fidelity_required=true

When config.md carries visual_fidelity_required: true, the Phasing skill
now documents an orchestrator-side precondition assertion that fires after
the review round but before writing status: approved to phasing.md.

Every UI-producing phase's acceptance criteria must cite at least one
wireframe artifact from design.md's visual-fidelity binding subsection.
Phases producing no UI work are exempt. On assertion failure the skill
writes a named diagnostic to reviews/phasing/visual-fidelity-precondition.md
(and stderr), leaves the status field unchanged, and exits non-zero.
When the flag is absent or false the assertion is entirely inert.

Three red-flag entries are added to reinforce the precondition boundary:
skipping the assertion, delegating it to a reviewer, and writing approval
despite a non-zero exit are all flagged as boundary violations.
